PostMates.com

They used all their HTML5 elements correctly, but left out the <nav> element. They just put a <ul> inside of the <header>. They do only have 3 nav options though. Some of the headers are also missing, but they're not actually needed in the site. Other than that the footer, header and sections are used correctly.

Creative9.com

Creative9 is development and interactive design agency based in the UK.

The <header> and <nav> elements are used properly, but the sections aren't outlined properly. This is mainly because of the use of <hgroup>. Only the first header of the group shows up in the outline. The rest of the untitled <section>'s are a result of the the content they contain. The <section>'s are used to separate or group the content, but not all of it requires it's own heading. They contain information and images that directly relate to the <h1> element. They could be placed into div's instead of sections.
